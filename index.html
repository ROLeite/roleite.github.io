About Me

Much of my research is motivated by questions in financial economics, though my work also deals in the fields of accounting, psychology and public policy. I am interested in understanding how humans make financial decisions and the roles of certain biases on the decision-making process, including how these biases may affect the performance of individuals and groups. My topics of interest also include the dynamics of financial institutions and disavantaged groups, especially regarding microfinance and microcredit. Finally, a new topic of research that I am pursuing is regarding the efficiency of public procurement processes and state-owned enterprises. My research has been published in journals in the fields of finance, business, and accounting such as the Journal of Public Administration Research and Theory, Journal of Business Ethics, The International Journal of Accounting, Accounting Forum, International Journal of Finance and Economics, and Finance Research Letters. I am also in the upper quartile of economic researchers in Brazil, in the last 10 years, at the RePEc ranking.
